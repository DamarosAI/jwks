Every trial decision should reconstruct.

Replay preserves protocol version, admitted evidence, criterion outcomes, review state, and adjudication history for every PASS, REVIEW, and FAIL across sites. This is not just citation. This is network execution memory.

Each replay packet is traceable and queryable from protocol lock through adjudication.

ProtocolProtocol version, criterion graph, and lock timestamp.
EvidenceAdmitted evidence set, source lineage, and missingness markers.
Engine runDeterministic run context, evaluation timestamp, and rule versions.
Criterion outcomesPASS, REVIEW, FAIL for every criterion with rationale references.
Review pathCoordinator actions, escalation steps, and PI adjudication decisions.
Integrity chainContent hash, signed lineage, and replay completeness status.
02_SIX_QUESTIONS

Six questions replay answers

  1. What protocol version was locked?
  2. What evidence was admitted?
  3. Which criterion passed, failed, or required review?
  4. Who adjudicated uncertainty?
  5. What changed?
  6. Can the decision be replayed?

Centerpiece contrast

04_REVIEW_WORKLIST

REVIEW is work

Replay captures REVIEW as structured operational work, not noise. Evidence blockers, human decisions, and final outcomes stay connected. Sponsors and CROs see where execution stalls before timelines collapse.

05_WHO_RELIES_ON_REPLAY

Who relies on replay

Site teams and coordinators run worklists. Sponsors, CROs, federal networks, coordinating centers, QA, IRB, and compliance teams inspect and defend decentralized trial-access execution without black-box eligibility.

Oversight becomes a query, not a fire drill.
