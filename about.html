I founded Damaros from inside the clinical environment.

Damaros is MD and MSAI led because clinical trial execution is not a generic automation problem. It is a regulated clinical workflow problem where protocol logic, site evidence, human judgment, privacy boundaries, and auditability determine whether AI can be trusted at all.

Clinical research is moving into an execution era. Discovery is accelerating, protocols are becoming more complex, patient evidence is expanding, and hospitals are being asked to operationalize work their software stack was never built to run.

That gap will become infrastructure.

I am an MD/MSAI candidate because this is the intersection I intend to build inside: medicine, AI systems, and institutional trust. From inside the system, the missing layer is visible before it becomes obvious from the outside.

The next clinical AI companies will not be built by wrapping models around workflows they do not understand. Regulated medicine does not adopt intelligence because it sounds powerful. It adopts systems that understand the work deeply enough to encode judgment, boundaries, provenance, and replay.

Clinical trials expose the failure first. They compress the hospital's execution problem into one chain: therapeutic intent, fragmented evidence, human review, institutional risk, and decisions reconstructed after the fact.

Trials are the first domain because the need is immediate, the cost is visible, and the workflow is already breaking. The larger system is the governed layer for complex clinical work.

Built from inside clinical medicine, the doctrine stays constant: the model is not the system of record.

Damaros is building the AI-native execution infrastructure for complex clinical work, beginning with clinical research.
