01_AI_SURFACE

What AI may touch

Permitted

  • Non-PHI protocol text, amendments, and sponsor documents.
  • Criterion structuring, ambiguity detection, and mapping suggestions via Trident.
  • Governance narration and operator support outside verdict issuance.
  • Policy-bound tasks with provenance and review hooks captured by Luna.

Prohibited

  • PHI-bearing patient evidence parsing on the screening path.
  • Eligibility verdict issuance, adjudication, or replay reconstruction by model output.
  • Audit source of truth generation from generative answers.
  • Any model as system of record for patient-path decisions.
Hard boundary. PHI never touches an LLM on the eligibility path. Optional external requests never serialize patient identifiers or cohort-linked fields.

04_TRIDENT_AND_LUNA

AI substrate with enforced separation

  • Trident compiles protocol language into executable eligibility structure. It sharpens with every protocol release and site run. It never issues a patient verdict.
  • Luna records AI origin, policy boundaries, provenance, and execution constraints. AI drafts language-heavy work upstream. Luna stamps what crossed the boundary and what did not.
05_CITATION_VS_REPLAY

Why replay beats citation

A citation shows what a model read. Replay reconstructs the decision an institution made: locked protocol version, admitted evidence, criterion outcomes, human adjudication, and referral packet state.

Sponsors, CROs, and federal research networks need decisions that reconstruct under scrutiny across community sites. Regulated AI does not scale through opacity. It scales through replay.

06_INTEGRATION_POSTURE

Enterprise trust extends to the wire

Clinic nodes integrate with Epic through SMART on FHIR Backend Services: signed JWT client assertions, public JWKS discovery, read-only cohort access, and server-side token handling. Signing keys live in secret stores. JWKS documents publish public material only. Private keys never appear in git, browser bundles, or JWKS JSON.

Helm deployments ship deny-by-default egress NetworkPolicy with explicit allowlists for Epic FHIR, OIDC, in-cluster data stores, and DNS. The trust boundary is architectural, not aspirational.

07_CATEGORY

Discovery vs governed access

Others validate AI-assisted trial discovery. Damaros builds the governed execution layer sponsors, CROs, and research networks need to operationalize and defend those decisions across community clinics. Discovery suggests. Governed execution proves.

08_VALIDATION

Validation dimensions

  • Criterion-level agreement, false PASS, false FAIL.
  • REVIEW burden and missing-evidence drivers.
  • Evidence-lineage completeness and replay completeness.
  • Adjudication deltas and run reproducibility for fixed protocol, cohort, and evaluation_as_of.
Validation covers execution integrity, not clinical efficacy claims. The same execution chain runs validation and production so evidence carries forward to cutover.
